Study on the perception of an inclusive phone design

2015


In this study that was part of my Master's course work, Amanda Magalhães and I analyzed a phone model produced by Brazilian company Intelbras to first find out if it fulfills the principles of Universal Design. Following that, we used the semantic differential method to compare the perception of the phone design between younger and older participants. In this method, participants are asked to rate the design based on adjective pairs - old/new, light/heavy, and so on.

We concluded that the product does follow Universal Design principles. After statistical analysis of the semantic differential surveys we also concluded that the design of the phone we tested made a good impression in both groups, with few differences being significant.

Usability Test of BurdaStyle

2014

During my master’s studies in Design, I planned and conducted a usability test for the sewing pattern magazine BurdaStyle, together with colleagues and professors. Our goal was to find out if it could be used by sewing beginners, and if there were any obstacles for them.

In our own lab setup, we had 12 participants try out 3 tasks that simulated the real use of the product. We collected qualitative and quantitative data on task completion rates, time on task, number of errors per task, comments, among others. After analysis, we concluded that there are many obstacles for beginners, such as specialized terms, lack of clear instructions, and too much information in a single pattern sheet, and also wrote recommendations.

As a result of this study, we wrote a paper that was presented and published in the 15o ERGODESIGN and USIHC. You can read the paper in full here. (In Portuguese)
