Research

Recruiting survey

Aim was to recruit possible candidates for the interviews, but also to have a broader pool of participants to collect simple quantitative data from.

Interviews

In order to understand the whys and hows that are so important for us in this study, remotely, we opted to do semi-structured interviews.

Challenges

A trade-off between deeper research and a more representative sample

Contextual inquiries in person would have been best for this, but having participants from all over Europe was also important, because each community works in a different way. We wouldn't be able to afford flying to different locations to meet all the participants, so we opted for doing interviews.

Workshop of Opportunities

Community workshop to discover innovative solutions

We came up with ideas to provide promoters a way to fulfil their unmet need, which is guaranteed reach in their local communities.

Through research, it became clear to us that creating a new app wouldn't solve their issues. They don't want to adopt new platforms if that doesn't mean they will have guaranteed reach right away.

So our proposed solution is around being a hub where event info and dancers converge, through collaboration with Forr√≥ Ambassadors around Europe. Together we discussed the viability of this model, and how to make it work.

Impact

For now, a proposal with a systemic approach that involves coordination from the Federation with the community promoters and curators, without creating any new apps.

Alignment between key community members: Federation, promoter, relevant events apps.

Research will also guide development and improvement of a soon-to-be-launched events app.
